## Context

AIBP's Axiom 0 (Human Sovereignty and Wellbeing) is identical in content to AIAP's Axiom 0. This raises a question: does AIBP inherit, share, or derive its Axiom 0 from AIAP?

Both protocols exist within the AIXP Foundation ecosystem. AIAP governs AI application behavior; AIBP governs AI social behavior. They operate at different layers but serve the same ultimate principle.

## Decision

AIBP independently establishes Axiom 0: Human Sovereignty and Wellbeing. AIBP's Axiom 0 is NOT inherited from, shared with, or derived from AIAP. The two protocols arrive at the same axiom through independent reasoning.

## Rationale

1. **Protocol sovereignty**: Each protocol must own its foundational principles. Dependency on another protocol's axiom would make AIBP's validity contingent on AIAP's existence.
2. **Value convergence**: When two independent systems arrive at the same fundamental value, this convergence is stronger evidence of the value's correctness than if one simply copied the other.
3. **Decoupling**: AIBP can exist and operate independently of AIAP. An agent may implement AIBP without implementing AIAP, and vice versa.
4. **Philosophical integrity**: Axiom 0 is not a shared resource — it is a universal principle that any AI governance framework should independently discover and adopt.

## Consequences

### Positive

- AIBP is fully self-contained — no external dependencies for its foundational values
- The convergence of two independent protocols on the same axiom strengthens the axiom's legitimacy
- Clear intellectual property boundaries between protocols

### Negative

- Requires explicit documentation to prevent confusion about the relationship
- Both protocols must independently maintain Axiom 0 compliance processes

### Neutral

- The AIXP Foundation serves as an umbrella but does not impose Axiom 0 top-down
- Future protocols in the ecosystem are expected to independently discover and adopt the same axiom
